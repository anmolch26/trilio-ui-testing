

Total Sales
Overview
Total Sales represents the overall revenue generated from all completed orders within a selected period.
 It serves as a key performance indicator of business health, reflecting the combined impact of marketing, pricing, product mix, and customer demand.
 Monitoring Total Sales helps assess growth trends, evaluate campaign effectiveness, and guide strategic decisions across sales, finance, and operations.

Formula
Total Sales = net_sales_amount + Shipping_amount + tax_amount

Insights and Actions
Revenue Growth Tracking — Identify periods of strong performance and potential slowdowns.


Product Performance Evaluation — Analyze Total Sales by product or category to spot bestsellers and underperformers.


Pricing & Discount Strategy — Sudden rises or drops indicate customer responses to pricing, promotions, or discounts.


Market Demand Insights — Seasonal spikes reveal changing customer demand patterns.


Profitability & Forecasting — Use Total Sales trends to support revenue forecasting and profit planning.



Detailed Breakdown
Net Sales = (Gross Sales - Returns - Discounts)
Shipping = SUM(shipping_amount)
Taxes = SUM(tax_amount)

Campaign

Device


Customer Type (New / Returning)



Related Metrics
Net Sales


Gross Sales

Tax

Example Use
Prompt:
What's the total sales from yesterday?
Response:
 Total Sales from Yesterday -  $120,450
Query:
SELECT SUM(net_sales_amount + Shipping_amount + tax_amount) AS total_sales
FROM orders
WHERE order_date BETWEEN :start AND :end;



Net Sales
Overview
Net Sales represent the actual revenue after deducting returns, refunds, and discounts from Gross Sales. It reflects the real income generated from completed transactions and provides a more accurate measure of sales performance.
Formula
   Net Sales = discount_amount - gross_sales

Insights and Actions
Profitability Tracking — Net Sales show how much revenue the business retains after adjustments.


Discount Efficiency — Identifies how discounts or returns affect real sales performance.


Customer Retention — A high return rate may signal product quality or satisfaction issues.


Sales Quality — Differentiates between inflated gross sales and sustainable net revenue.


Campaign Evaluation — Useful to measure how much revenue promotions actually retain.


Detailed Breakdown
Gross Sales = sum(discount_amount) + sum(net_sales)


Discounts = sum(discount_amount)


Suggested Dimensions
Campaign · Product / SKU · Channel · Customer Type · Region
Related Metrics
Gross Sales · Discount Rate · Total Sales · MER · ROAS
Example Use
Prompt: How much was our net sales yesterday?
 Response:
 Net Sales from Yesterday — $115,500
Query
SELECT SUM(net_sales_amount) As net_sales
FROM orders
WHERE order_date BETWEEN :start AND :end;




Gross Sales
Overview
Gross Sales represents the total revenue generated from all completed orders before any deductions such as returns, discounts, or allowances. It serves as a key measure of overall sales volume and business scale, providing a clear view of top-line performance.
Formula
Gross Sales = Net Sales  + Discount Amount

Insights and Actions
Revenue Momentum — Tracking Gross Sales helps identify sales growth trends and the impact of marketing campaigns.


Market Expansion — High Gross Sales across new regions or channels indicate strong market adoption.


Product Demand — Useful for evaluating which products or categories generate the highest raw revenue.


Benchmarking — Acts as a baseline for comparing net sales, profit margins, and return rates.


Forecasting — Helps anticipate inventory and production needs based on gross sales trends.


Detailed Breakdown
Net Sales= discount_amount - gross_sales


Discount amount = discount_amount


Suggested Dimensions
Channel · Campaign · Product / SKU · Category · Region · Time Period
Related Metrics
Net Sales · Total Sales · Discount Rate · Units Sold · AOV
Example Use
Prompt: What were the gross sales for last week?
 Response:
 Gross Sales from Last Week — $180,000




Query

SELECT SUM(net_sales_amount + discount_amount) As gross_sales
FROM orders
WHERE order_date BETWEEN :start AND :end;

ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)
Overview
Return on Ad Spend (ROAS) measures the revenue generated per dollar spent on advertising. It provides a focused view of campaign-level profitability and efficiency.
Formula
ROAS = Ads Revenue ÷ Advertising Spend

Insights and Actions
Campaign Efficiency — Identifies which ads are generating profitable returns.


Performance Optimization — Helps reallocate budgets to top-performing campaigns.


ROI Measurement — Quantifies how marketing spend translates into sales.


Benchmarking — Compare ROAS across products, platforms, or timeframes.


Forecasting — Helps predict returns from future ad investments.


Detailed Breakdown
Revenue from Ads = sum(sales_attributed_to_ads)


Ad Spend = sum(spend_amount)


Suggested Dimensions
Channel · Campaign · Product · Region · Device
Related Metrics
MER · Ad Spend · Total Sales · Profit Margin
Example Use
Prompt: What’s our ROAS for Facebook campaigns this week?
 Response:
 ROAS for Facebook Campaigns — 4.3
Query

SELECT SUM(COALESCE(conversions_value, 0.0)) / NULLIF(SUM(COALESCE(spend, 0.0)), 0) As roas
FROM spends
WHERE spends_date BETWEEN :start AND :end;

Discount Rate
Overview
Discount Rate measures the percentage of revenue reduced through discounts relative to gross sales. It’s essential for understanding promotional impact and margin control.
Formula
Discount Rate = (Total Discount Amount ÷ Gross Sales) × 100

Insights and Actions
Promotion Effectiveness — Track how discounts influence sales volume.


Margin Management — Identify if heavy discounts are hurting profitability.


Customer Behavior — Evaluate if discounts are driving repeat purchases.


Pricing Strategy — Optimize discount levels for maximum ROI.


Trend Analysis — Compare discount rates across periods to detect overuse.


Detailed Breakdown
Total Discounts = sum(discount_amount)


Gross Sales = sum(order_amount)


Suggested Dimensions
Campaign · Product / SKU · Channel · Customer Type · Region
Related Metrics
Gross Sales · Net Sales · Total Sales · AOV
Example Use
Prompt: What was the discount rate last month?
 Response:
 Discount Rate for Last Month — 8.7%


Query


SELECT  SUM(discount_amount) * 100.0/ NULLIF(SUM(net_sales_amount + discount_amount), 0),0) AS discount_rate
FROM orders
WHERE order_date BETWEEN :start AND :end;

Cancellation Rate
Overview
Cancellation Rate measures the percentage of orders canceled after being placed. It’s a key performance metric that indicates order fulfillment reliability, customer satisfaction, and operational efficiency.
Formula
Cancellation Rate = (Canceled Orders ÷ Total Orders) × 100.

Insights and Actions
Customer Satisfaction — High cancellation rates may signal poor user experience or product issues.


Operational Efficiency — Helps identify fulfillment or stock-related delays leading to cancellations.


Demand Forecasting — Frequent cancellations may distort true demand forecasts.


Platform Reliability — Useful for evaluating payment or logistics performance.


Policy Impact — Tracks how flexible return/cancellation policies affect overall business.


Detailed Breakdown
Total Cancellations = count(cancelled_orders)


Total Orders = count(all_orders)


Suggested Dimensions
Channel · Product / SKU · Region · Campaign · Customer Type
Related Metrics
Total Orders · Total Cancellations · Conversion Rate · Return Rate
Example Use
Prompt: What’s our cancellation rate for this week?
 Response:
 Cancellation Rate for This Week — 3.4%
Query


SELECT  COALESCE(COUNT(DISTINCT order_id) * 100.0 /NULLIF(COUNT(DISTINCT order_id), 0),0) AS cancellation_rate
FROM orders
WHERE order_date BETWEEN :start AND :end;

ACOS (Advertising Cost of Sales)
Overview
Advertising Cost of Sales (ACOS) measures the percentage of ad spend relative to the revenue generated from those ads. It’s the inverse of ROAS and indicates advertising efficiency.
Formula
ACOS = (Ad Spend) ÷ (conversions_value) × 100

Insights and Actions
Efficiency Check — Lower ACOS means better ad profitability.


Budget Control — Helps ensure ad spending is aligned with expected returns.


Campaign Comparison — Compare efficiency across campaigns or keywords.


Trend Analysis — Monitor changes over time for optimization.


ROI Targeting — Supports defining profitable advertising thresholds.


Detailed Breakdown
Ad Spend = sum(spend_amount)


Ad Revenue = sum(sales_from_ads)


Suggested Dimensions
Campaign · Channel · Product / SKU · Region · Device
Related Metrics
ROAS · MER · Ad Spend · Conversion Rate
Example Use
Prompt: What was our ACOS last month?
 Response:
 ACOS for Last Month — 18.5%
Query


SELECT ROUND((SUM(COALESCE(spend, 0.0)) / NULLIF(SUM(COALESCE(conversions_value, 0.0)), 0)) * 100,2 ) AS acos
FROM spends
WHERE spends_date BETWEEN :start AND :end;

Overview
Cost Per Click (CPC) measures the average cost incurred for each ad click. It’s essential for evaluating paid media efficiency and bid strategy.

Cost Per Click (CPC) = Total Spend ÷ Total Number of Clicks

Performance Tracking — Compare CPC across platforms and campaigns.


Budget Allocation — Direct funds to lower-cost, high-converting ads.


ROI Analysis — Use alongside ROAS or Conversion Rate for complete ad insight.


Detailed Breakdown
Total Ad Spend = sum(spend_amount)


Total Clicks = sum(clicks)


Suggested Dimensions
Campaign · Channel · Keyword · Region · Device
Related Metrics
Ad Spend · Clicks · ROAS · ACOS
Example Use
Prompt: What’s our average CPC for the week?
 Response:
 Average CPC for This Week — $0.72
Query


SELECT ROUND(CASE WHEN SUM(clicks) > 0 THEN SUM(spend) / NULLIF(SUM(clicks), 0)ELSE 0 END, 2) AS cost_per_click
FROM spends
WHERE spends_date BETWEEN :start AND :end;

 Returning Customer Rate = (Number of Returning Customers ÷ Total Unique Customers) × 100

Insights and Actions
Loyalty Tracking — Indicates brand stickiness and satisfaction.


Revenue Stability — Repeat customers often contribute to consistent income.


Retention Strategy — Evaluate loyalty programs and engagement tactics.


Customer Segmentation — Identify repeat vs. one-time buyers.


Growth Forecasting — High returning rate supports sustainable growth.


Detailed Breakdown
Returning Customers = count(distinct repeat_customer_ids)


Total Customers = count(distinct customers)


Suggested Dimensions
Region · Campaign · Customer Type · Channel
Related Metrics
Churn Rate · CAC · LTV · Conversion Rate
Example Use
Prompt: What’s our returning customer rate this quarter?
 Response:
 Returning Customer Rate — 29.4%


Query
SELECT ROUND(COUNT(DISTINCT CASE WHEN user_type = 'returning_customer' THEN customer_email END)::decimal * 100/ NULLIF(COUNT(DISTINCT customer_email), 0),4)END AS returning_customer_rate
FROM orders
WHERE orders_date BETWEEN :start AND :end;

SELECT  COALESCE(ROUND(((a.spends) / NULLIF((s.ads_net_sales), 0)) * 100, 2), 0.0) AS tacos
FROM spends
WHERE spends_date BETWEEN :start AND :end;
